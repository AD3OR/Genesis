Angelman Syndrome

Angelman syndrome is a rare genetic disorder that primarily affects the nervous system. It is characterized by severe developmental delays, speech impairments, movement and balance problems, seizures, and a typically happy demeanor with frequent smiling and laughter. It is caused by a problem with the UBE3A gene on chromosome 15.

Common Symptoms

Severe developmental delays (Cerebellar)

Frequent smiling and happy demeanor

Movement and balance disorders

Epileptic seizures
